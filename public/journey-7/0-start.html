Apply for help with fees

You may not have to pay a court or tribunal fee, or you may get some money off.


You're eligible to apply if:

You have less than £3,000 in savings and investments

You're receiving one of these benefits:

  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Universal Credit (and you’re earning less than £6,000 a year)
  • State Pension (Guarantee Credit)
  • Scottish Civil Legal Aid (not Advice and Assistance or Advice by Way of Representation)

We'll check you're receiving these benefits with the Department for Work and Pensions.

Your total monthly income is less than £1,085

Income can include benefits such as working tax credit and child tax credit.

See more about what you should include as income.

The information you provide about your income will act as evidence to support your application.


See more detailed information about who can apply for help with fees.

What you need to apply:

  • the court or tribunal form name or number for your claim
    (the form name is often at the top of the form; the number at the bottom)
  • your case, claim or ‘notice to pay’ number
    (find this on letters from the court or tribunal, or leave blank if your claim hasn't started yet)
  • your National Insurance number
  • your most recent month’s bank statement for all current accounts
  • your most recent payslip (or tax return if you are self employed)

Applying takes around 10 minutes. This service is for England and Wales only.
