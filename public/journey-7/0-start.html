Apply for help with fees

You may not have to pay a court or tribunal fee, or you may get some money off.

  You're likely to get help

You have less than £3,000 in savings and investments

Your total monthly income is less than £1,085 a month

You're receiving one of these benefits:
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Universal Credit (and you’re earning less than £6,000)
  • State Pension (Guarantee Credit)
  • Scottish Civil Legal Aid (not Advice and Assistance or Advice by Way of Representation)

  You may still get help

You have more than £3,000 in savings and investments

Your total monthly income is more than £1,085 a month

You're receiving one of these benefits:
  • Child Benefit
  • Working Tax Credit
  • Child Tax Credit
  • Contribution-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Contribution-based Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Universal Credit (and you’re earning more than £6,000)

For more information, read the guide  How to apply for help with fees (PDF 145KB)

  You won't get help

You have more than £16,000 in savings and investments

Your total monthly income is more than £5,735 a month


What you'll need to apply:

  • the form name or number of the court or tribunal form that you’re using
    (You can usually find the form name at the top of the form, and the number at the bottom)
  • your case, claim or ‘notice to pay’ number. (if you have one)
    (If your case is ongoing then you’ll find the reference number on letters from the court or tribunal)
  • your National Insurance number (if you have one)
  • you most recent month’s bank statement for any and all current accounts
  • your most recent payslip or tax return if you are self employed
  • any letters about the benefits you are receiving

Applying takes around 10 minutes.

Before you start
