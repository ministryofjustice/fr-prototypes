Apply for help with fees

You may not have to pay a court or tribunal fee, or you may get some money off.

You can get help if you:
  • only have a small amount of savings and investments
  • and receive certain benefits (see below)
  • or are on a low income

Benefits
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Universal Credit (and you’re earning less than £6,000)
  • State Pension (Guarantee Credit)
  • Scottish Civil Legal Aid (not Advice and Assistance or Advice by Way of Representation)
To use this service you'll need:
  • your National Insurance number (if you have one)
  • the form name or number of the court or tribunal form that you’re using. (You can usually find the form name at the top of the form, and the number at the bottom.)
  • your case, claim or ‘notice to pay’ number. (if you have one. If your case is ongoing then you’ll find the reference number on letters from the court or tribunal.)
  • details about your income and any benefits you are receiving

Applying takes around 10 minutes.

Before you start
