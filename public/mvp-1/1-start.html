Apply for help with fees

You may not have to pay a court or tribunal fee, or you may get some money off.

Who can get help with fees?

You can apply for help with fees if you:
  • only have a small amount of savings and investments
  • and receive certain benefits
  • or are on a low income

Help with fees is sometimes known as ‘fee remission’.

Usually only individuals can apply for help with fees. There are some exceptions, for example:
  • sole traders (people who run their own business)
  • charities and not-for-profit organisations who are making a case to the UK Supreme Court
  • companies applying to the Gambling Appeals Tribunal

How to apply

You will need to apply by post using the ‘Apply for help with fees (EX160)’ (PDF 82KB).

For more information on how to apply, you can read the guide 'Guide apply for help with court, tribunal and probate fees (EX160A)' (PDF 145KB).

This service is for England and Wales only.
