Apply for help with fees

You may not have to pay a court or tribunal fee, or you may get some money off.

Check if you can get help with fees


1. Do you have less than £3000 in savings and investments?

2. Do you receive any of these benefits?

  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Universal Credit (and you’re earning less than £6,000 a year)
  • State Pension (Guarantee Credit)
  • Scottish Civil Legal Aid (not Advice and Assistance or Advice by Way of Representation)

3. Do you earn less than £1000 a month before tax and National Insurance?

  • 'Yes' to 2 or more questions: you're likely to get help with your fees. This is not a final decision.
  • 'No' to 2 or more questions: we need to know more about you. Your age, relationship status and court fee will all be considered.

Read more about who is eligible

Apply now

  • You need your National Insurance number and details of your savings and any income to hand. (eg payslips and tax returns)
  • Complete the application form: ‘Apply for help with fees (EX160)’(PDF 82KB).
  • Send the completed form to the court or tribunal dealing with your case or claim. See information about courts and tribunals including contact details.

Read the guide to applying for help with fees (PDF 145KB) for more information.
