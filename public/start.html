Apply for help with court and tribunal fees

You may not have to pay a fee, or you may get some money off if you have a small amount of savings, and you're on a low income or receiving certain benefits.


To be eligible for help with fees, you need to show that:


  1. You (and your partner if you have one) have a small amount of savings
    More about maximum savings amounts

    In most cases you (and your partner if you have one) should have less than £3,000 in savings and investments if you’re under 61. If you’re 61 or over, you can have up to £16,000.

    Savings and investments don't include your main home, personal belongings, wages or benefits.

  2. And you’re either on benefits
    There are 6 qualifying benefits
    If you receive 1 of these benefits (and have a small amount of savings) you won't have to pay a fee:
    • Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
    • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
    • Income Support
    • Universal Credit
    • Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit)
    • Scottish Civil Legal Aid
  3. or have a low income.
    More about what qualifies as a low income

    If you earn less a month than the amounts in the table below (before tax and National Insurance has been taken off) then you won't have to pay a fee. This can include benefits, eg working tax credit, child tax credit.

    Number of childrenIf you're singleIf you have a partner
    0£1,085£1,245
    1£1,330£1,490
    2£1,575£1,735

    If you have 3 or more children there’s an extra allowance of £245 for each child.

    See the guide for detailed information about who is eligible to apply for help with fees.


What you'll need:

  • National Insurance number
  • court or tribunal form name or number
  • case, claim or ‘notice to pay’ number (if you have one)
  • most recent bank statement for all current accounts
  • most recent payslip (if you have one)
  • most recent tax return (if you're self employed)

Applying takes around 10 minutes. This service is for England and Wales only.
