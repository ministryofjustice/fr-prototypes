About getting help with court and tribunal fees


Individuals can apply for financial help with their court or tribunal fees if they:
  • Individuals can apply for financial help with their court or tribunal fees if they:
  • only have a small amount of savings and investments
  • and receive certain benefits
  • or are on a low income

This service used to be known as ‘fee remission’.
Processing applications for help with fees

You can process all applications using the online service. The service has a link to the Department for Work and Pensions so can check whether applicants are receiving benefits.

When you’ve entered all the information from the form you’ll get a result about whether the applicant is eligible for help with fees.

You’ll also be prompted to send a letter requesting further information or part-payment if the applicant hasn’t fully completed the form, or is eligible to pay part of the fee.
Evidence

In most cases you won’t need to check paper evidence (eg bank statements and payslips). However, some applications are chosen to have evidence checked. When this happens, you’ll be prompted to write to the applicant asking to see evidence of their income. A letter will be generated for you to use.

Applications will move to ‘Applications waiting for evidence’ until you receive the evidence.
Setting up your account

You’ll need to be set up with an account to process help with fees applications using the online tool at: URL HERE

Your team manager is usually responsible for setting up your account.

You’ll receive an email with a link to set up your password. Once you’ve done this, you’re ready to start processing
Updating your details

You can update your:
  • name
  • jurisdiction
  • office
  • password

Click on your name in the top right hand corner of the page to view and update your details.

Your role will be set to ‘staff’ or ‘manager’. This can only be changed by another manager. Staff can process applications, managers can add and remove staff members as well as process applications.

Your email address can’t be changed. This is for security reasons. If you need to use a different email address, then you’ll need to have a new account set up.

Before you start
  1. Check the statement of truth has been signed in question 12. Unsigned forms should be returned to the applicant
  2. Check the applicant is not: ul li on the list of vexatious litigants [LINK: gov.uk/guidance/vexatious-litigants] li bound by a general civil restraint order (CRO) [LINK: gov.uk/guidance/general-civil-restraint-orders-in-force] li bound by an extended civil restraint order (CRO) [LINK: gov.uk/guidance/extended-civil-restraint-orders-in-force] li bound by a restrictions of proceedings order (RPO) [LINK TO COME] li receiving Legal Aid, see more about Legal Aid [LINK TO POLICY] li a company (excluding companies applying to the Gambling Appeals Tribunal) see more about exceptions for companies [LINK TO POLICY] li a charity or not for profit organisation (excluding those making a case to the UK Supreme Court) see more about exceptions for charities and non-profit organisations [LINK TO POLICY]
  3. Check the case or claim is not: ul li either a money claim online (MCOL) or possession claim online (PCOL) (including refunds) li a request for duplicate documents or search fees (unless the applicant did not receive the originals or had no fixed address when an order was made)
National Insurance (NI) number

If the applicant has answered ‘Yes’ to question 9, but has not provided their NI number in question 1, contact them by phone to ask for it.

You should continue to process the application as usual. If you’re not able to contact the applicant, you can write to them. A draft letter will be generated for you to use when you’ve completed processing.
Refunds
  • Check the date the fee was paid in the case management system
  • Check the fee hasn’t already been refunded
Emergency cases
  • suspending an eviction
  • debtor insolvency petition
  • involving children or vulnerable adults
  • involving domestic violence
  • an injunction
  • ‘out of hours’ provisions at the Royal Courts of Justice
Total monthly income
  • If the applicant is part of a couple they must have provided details of their partner’s income
  • Add the 2 monthly income amounts and enter the total

Some benefits shouldn't be included as income, eg Carer's Allowance, Housing Benefit or childcare element of Working Tax Credit, see the full list of excluded benefits [LINK TO POLICY]
