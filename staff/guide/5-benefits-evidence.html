Paper evidence

You may need to check paper evidence to confirm the applicant is receiving benefits. This is usually an official letter.

For example when:
  • the applicant hasn't provided their National Insurance number
  • the applicant recently started receiving benefits but the Department for Work and Pensions is not up to date
  • the applicant receives joint benefits and the benefit is in the applicant's partner's name

Otherwise you'll need to write to the applicant and ask them for paper evidence.
